My Candidate Description

{% include back.html %}

Thank you for your interest and for clicking through to this page. The fact that you are here means a lot to me.

You are here because I sent you this link in response to an email, LinkedIn message, or a message you sent me. I sincerely appreciate your taking the time to come here and read this page. The fact that you did shows that you are not simply dialling for dollars or using a "spray and pray" approach to finding candidates.

I understand that this page may come as arrogant, cocky, or flippant; I would like to stress that this isn't my intention. As you are probably aware, I receive a lot of messages from people looking for software developers to fill various roles. This page is mostly to save me time in answering this stream of messages, but also a polite reminder that we aren't playing by the old rules anymore. Gone are the days where a company would advertise a position and put applicants through a gruelling process so that one "lucky" candidate would prove themselves worthy and be offered the job.

This page is meant as a screen for you or the company you're working with, and to save each other time. If you or your company can live with the ground rules below, and if what I am looking for matches the position you're working to fill, then I'd be happy to talk further.

Profile

I am an experienced software developer and technologist with DevOps exposure who can speak French and English fluently. The majority of my programming experience is with JavaScript (NodeJS and in the browser) and Ruby, building web applications for companies like Rangle and Samsung.

Linux (Ubuntu) is my desktop operating system, I am proficient in using the command line to get things done. I am familiar with the Agile framework and with working in sprints or using kanban. I have deployed, configured, and looked after several servers, I am comfortable with writing iptables rules or partitioning a hard drive.

My background studying business administration both in Canada and Poland gives me valuable insights as to the value behind the code I write, and how to balance business goals with engineering goals. Efficiency is important to me, I enjoy thinking about how things are done, why, and how could they be made better while saving time, money, and sanity.

My passion for working with technology extends beyong the workplace. I have a number of open source projects on GitHub, NPM, Docker Hub, and Ansible Galaxy; I run several self-hosted services on my home server and enjoy tinkering with new technologies.

I am allowed to live and work in Canada and in the European Union without additional paperwork or sponsorship.

What I am looking for

I am looking for a mature company that is past the issues that come with scaling up its team. That is, a company where there are clearly defined expectations and processes for onboarding new team members (both technically and organizationally), for building and following a product roadmap, and for releasing new versions or features.

My experience spans both the frontend and the backend, I have a strong preference for writing logic rather than CSS. My style is well tested and functional code, ramda is one of my favorite libraries.

I work best with teams that are collaborative, diverse, inclusive, where technical decisions are made based on everyone's input, and where making mistakes is ok.

System administration and infrastructure are of interest to me, and I would enjoy touching on these areas.

Ground rules for interviewing and regarding working conditions

  • Must actively observe or encourage clean coding practices like TDD, CI/CD, fixing existing major bugs before writing new features, continuous refactoring, code reviews, technical debt repayment, etc. or want to bring me in with a mandate to get your team to do these things.
  • Occasional work from home or remote work is possible and acceptable.
  • Must not practice "Arbitrary Deadline Driven Development", and be open to either reduce the deliverables' scope or move the deadline as appropriate.
  • Contracting strongly preferred over full time salaried positions.
  • Strictly no whiteboarding, brain-teasers, riddles, or algorithm-centric interview questions. I'm more than happy to have a technical discussion around real-world situations relevant to the job, or talk about my open-source code. Pair programming is ok as long as it's pair programming and doesn't involve the kind of questions mentioned above.
  • I will sign an NDA to protect your intellectual property and agree not to distribute it to competitors. I won't agree to anything restricting my ability to do freelance projects or claiming ownership over the code I write on my own time using my own equipment and unrelated to your business.
  • I won't agree to a no-compete clause unless it comes with a lump sum to match. That is, if you would like to impose a six month no-compete period, then there has to be a lump sum payable if the no-compete applies to cover the period during which I won't be allowed to work.
  • I work 40 hours weeks, occasional overtime is ok if production is down and everything is on fire. I am not available to work or attend work-related matters outside of regular business hours unless on-call and paid for the time on-call.
  • My programming tools of choice are free and open source (vim, Linux), I would much rather work using an enterprise class ThinkPad than a MacBook Pro.
  • Adtech, defence, and social media related software or products are not things I am interested in building or contributing to.
  • If your perks/benefits mostly consist of items like "free snacks", "open office space", "ping-pong/foosball table", or "regular company hackathons", we're probably not a good fit for each other.
  • I don't have a ".doc" version of my resume. I generate a PDF from LaTeX markup, and the PDF version is the only available one.
  • I will not disclose my current or past salaries, no matter how much you insist. If your software requires a number to proceed, I'd be happy to look at how I could help fix your defective software.

Thank You

Thank you for humoring me, reading all the way through, and paying attention.

I believe in failing fast, and this page is putting that principle into action by candidly laying out my expectations. I also believe in optimizing processes, and I am convinced this page will save both of us a lot of time over the long term.

I hope this has given you enough insight whether it makes sense to continue talking. If so, we can continue the discussion here. If not, thank you for making it to the end; I wish you all the best and good luck.
